Neurodivergency on Campus

Exploratory qualitative interviews.

  • Span: September to October, 2024
  • Status: Completed

Problem Statement

Neurodivergent students often face barriers in accessing appropriate resources, which can impact their well-being and academic success.

Goal of Study

This study seeked to explore challenges faced by neurodivergent students, as well as analyze the effectiveness of current mental and academic support services already in place at the University of Michigan.

Methodology

Photo-elicitation (the use of visual stimuli to capture information) was used to conduct qualitative interviews. Thematic analyses of participant transcripts were then coded into an affinity diagram.

The Interview Process

Five participants consented to virtual 45-minute interviews. Using an interview guide made on Google Docs, and a curated set of ten photos on academic and social life, I prompted the participants to reflect on their personal experiences and challenges.

Coding and Analysis

Interview transcripts were then reviewed using thematic analysis, i.e examining data to identify reoccuring themes. Coding, or organizing data sets with labels, was done through Google Docs. Key patterns and themes were later organized into an affinity diagram, shown below. This visual map organized the data into three overarching themes that each reference direct quotations from the interviewees.

Findings

Participants reported depression and anxiety, worsened by challenges adjusting to the large, overstimulating campus. Several faced difficulties with traditional talk therapy provided by the campus, feeling the need to "mask”, despite the therapeutic environment, due to therapists' limited understanding of neurodivergence. Executive dysfunction, communication barriers, and the absence of supportive communities further compounded their struggles.

Conclusion

Given limitations of time, and the conceptual nature of this project, the findings are not suitable for statistical analysis due to the sample size and lack of diversity (e.g., all participants were female). However, the study successfully surfaced qualitative insights into how neurodivergent students navigate their environments and highlighted unmet needs in their academic and social lives. These findings provided important insights that can inform future initiatives to improve resources and, most importantly, foster a more supportive experience for neurodivergent students.
